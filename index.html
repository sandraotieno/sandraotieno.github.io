Data Analytics
Data analytics is the systematic exploration of raw data using statistical methods, coding, and algorithmic techniques to uncover trends, patterns, and relationships. It transforms large and small datasets into measurable evidence that explains what happened, why it happened, and what is likely to happen next. It spans descriptive, diagnostic, predictive and prescriptive analysis, enabling teams to quantify behaviour, model scenarios and support data-driven actions across products, operations and strategy.
Core tools: Python, R, SQL, SPSS, Stata, Jupyter Notebook.
Business Intelligence
Business intelligence is the development of integrated data systems, dashboards and reporting pipelines that convert complex organisational data into consistent, real-time visibility. It focuses on building reliable data infrastructure, automated metrics, standardised KPIs and interactive visual tools that help teams monitor performance, identify deviations and align decisions with business goals.
Core tools: Power BI (DAX, Power Query), Tableau, Looker Studio, ETL/ELT data models.
Insight Generation
Insight generation is the interpretation stage where analytical findings are translated into meaning, narratives and strategic recommendations. It goes beyond numbers to explain implications, opportunities, risks and human-centred drivers behind the data. It connects patterns to real-world behaviour, synthesises evidence into stories and offers actionable guidance that influences decisions, innovation and business strategy.
Core tools: Excel, PowerPoint, survey analysis frameworks, PDF and deck reporting.
