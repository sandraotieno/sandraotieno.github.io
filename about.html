About me

Who I am

I am a data analytics and business intelligence professional with more than six years of experience helping organisations interpret their markets, customers and performance. I have worked across banking, FMCG, telecoms, retail, NGOs and development programs in multiple African markets.

My strength lies in understanding the business context behind each question, shaping practical analytical approaches and delivering outputs that leaders can trust. I enjoy working closely with analysts, engineers and decision makers so that the technical work always connects back to real decisions and trade offs.

I hold a Master of Science in Data Science and Analytics and a Bachelor of Economics and Statistics. This combination of analytical depth and business grounding allows me to move comfortably between statistical work, modelling, BI development and strategic reporting.

My focus is always on clarity, accuracy and insight that moves from data to understanding to stories that leaders can use with confidence.

How I do it
  • 1. Start with data collection and structure I shape survey instruments, sampling plans and structured input templates, then bring together raw files from survey platforms, Excel sheets, transactional systems and operational data sources.
  • 2. Clean, prepare and validate datasets I use SQL, Python, R, SPSS, Stata and Power Query to clean, reshape and validate data, creating stable ELT and ETL ready tables for modelling and business intelligence.
  • 3. Explore, analyze and model I run exploratory analysis, segmentation, forecasting, classification and credit or behavioural models using Pandas, PySpark, Scikit Learn, R procedures, SPSS and Stata.
  • 4. Build BI structures and metric layers I design star schemas, metric layers and ELT and ETL workflows using SQL, dbt and Power Query to support dashboards and reports in Power BI, Tableau and Looker Studio.
  • 5. Translate results into decision ready stories I develop visual narratives, concise summaries and executive presentations that show what the numbers mean, why the findings matter and which decisions they support.
